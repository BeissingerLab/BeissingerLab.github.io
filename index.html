Welcome to the Beissinger Lab. We study plant quantitative and population genetics to better understand how plants have evolved in the past, how phenotypes and genotypes are related in the present, and how this information can be used for plant improvement in the future. Our research is focussed on the maize (corn) genome. Our primary interests fall into two categories:

  1. We are interested in understanding how organisms respond to evolutionary forces such as artificial selection.
  2. We are working to achieve an improved understanding of complexities of genotype-phenotype relationships such as interactions and non-additivity.

We are supported by the USDA-ARS at the University of Missouri, Columbia.
